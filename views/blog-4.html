Prepare for Aptitude tests: A Guide

How to prepare for Aptitude tests?

The aptitude test is the first screening test used to filter students based on their logical ability, decision-making skills, and personality. The aptitude tests help the employers to assess the skills and abilities of candidates as it reflects their capacity to do the job. It could be either an online one or a paper-based test.
Generally, the aptitude tests consist of quantitative aptitude, logical reasoning, and verbal reasoning. They are multiple-choice questions and are conducted to assess the candidate’s creativity, innovation, problem-solving skills and precision.

The Syllabus for Aptitude tests asked for the companies tests :

1. Quantitative Aptitude : It consists of chapters like Permutations and Combinations, Probability, Profit and Loss, Time and Distance, Interest, Time and Work, and Problems on trains.
2. Logical Reasoning: Chapters like Analogy, Seating Arrangements, Puzzles, Blood Relations, and Coding-Decoding are of very high weightage.
3. Verbal Reasoning: This part tests a candidate's Vocabulary, Analogy, and Sentence Correction.

Please note that:
1. Don't get stuck on one question: You should decide which questions you should complete first and which questions you should skip.
2. Don’t Guess: Most of the exams have negative marking so don’t guess the answers.
3. Practice more and more: Don’t practice until you get it right, practice till you can’t get it wrong.
4. Don’t Panic: Take a deep breath before starting the paper and stay calm.
5. Pay more attention to the concept : Concept is power. You can solve every type of question if your concepts are clear.
6.Importance of Accuracy: Even if your test doesn’t have negative marking never make wild guesses as accuracy matters a lot.
