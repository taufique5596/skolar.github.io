Crack tricky INTERVIEW QUESTIONS in no time

The questions listed below are the most common and most tricky ones asked in an interview from an employer.

1. If hired, how long are you planning to work here?
From this question, they want to know if you are planning for short term recruitment or long term training new employees. It is a time-consuming and hectic process.
Expected answer: If hired, I would like to work with XYZ company in the long term.

2. Why do you want to work here?
You have to tell them about the company's positive things like their mission, culture, and positive work environment in this question.
Expected answer: It would be a great opportunity for me to work with the company ABC. I have always wanted to work with a company having a positive work environment.

3. “Out of all the candidates, why should we hire you?
This is one of the most asked tricky questions. This is the best chance to tell the interviewee about your strengths, skills and most important tell them about your uniqueness. So craft the response according to job you are applying for.
If you are a fresher tell them about the projects that you have done.
Expected answer: you should hire me as a software developer as I possess all the skills and experience that you're looking for. I'm pretty confident that I am the best candidate for this job role.

4. Tell me how other people would describe you?
This is one of the most difficult questions, you have to be crafty to frame an answer for this question. But always tell the truth.

5. What motivates you ?
Here you need not to panic, you can pinpoint all the this that made your eyes light up when you read about the job. While answering this question you can relate it with any past experience that you have and be honest.
Expected answer : I am always eager to learn new things and take new responsibilities so that I am constantly learning and contributing more to my team and organization. And I am the kind of person who always thrives on hitting and exceeding goals and on friendly competition.

6. What makes you unique?
Hey! It’s a good sign. Tell them about your positive qualities.
Understand the job and keep the specific role you are applying for. Emphasize the skills that they are actually looking for.
Expected answer: What makes me unique is that I basically taught myself animation from scratch. I was immediately drawn to it in college, and with the limited resources available to me, I decided to take matters into my own hands—and that’s the approach I take in all aspects of my work as a video editor. I don’t just wait around for things to happen, and when I can, I’m always eager to step in and take on new projects, pick up new skills, or brainstorm new ideas.”(change it)
